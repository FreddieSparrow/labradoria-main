The Founding Fathers

The Republic of Labradoria was conceived and established in 2024 by two founding fathers whose vision, initiative, and shared values gave rise to this sovereign micronation.

Founding Father & Head of State

Freddie Sparrow

1st President of the Republic of Labradoria

Freddie Sparrow is the founding President of the Republic of Labradoria, serving as Head of State and Head of Government since the Republic's formal constitution in 2026. As the driving force behind the establishment of Labradoria, President Sparrow has overseen the drafting of the Constitution, the publication of the Territorial Register, and the formal recognition of sovereign states on behalf of the Republic.

President Sparrow's vision for Labradoria is of a peaceful, Catholic, and law-governed micronation open to all who share its values — a republic built on faith, community, and the common good.

Founding Father & Minister of Technology

Adam Calcroft

Co-Founder & Minister of Science, Technology & Innovation
CEO, T&A 3D Printing  ·  Creator, TechTesting  ·  Founder, CookieCloud / CookieHost / CookieNet

Adam Calcroft is a co-founder of the Republic of Labradoria and serves as Minister of Foreign Affairs and Territorial Affairs. A self-described networking addict and infrastructure builder, Adam began building serious technical systems at age 10 — long before most people his age had considered what a subnet is.

Adam is the creator of CookieCloud, a fully self-built, self-hosted distributed cloud platform running on the CookieNet — his bedroom datacentre. CookieCloud provides cloud storage, encrypted secret sharing, password management, photo management, cloud meetings, collaborative whiteboards, and more — all built, operated, and maintained by Adam personally. It runs on a custom Ubuntu-based OS he calls Cookibuntu, using Docker, Kubernetes, and NGINX across a distributed server cluster, with 100% Ubiquiti networking and a leased-line uplink via Zen Internet with a public IPv4 range and a /46 IPv6 block. CookieNet aims to be fully carbon neutral by 2030; all cooling is closed-loop with zero water usage.

From CookieCloud grew CookieHost, a server hosting platform offering game hosting, web hosting, code hosting, database hosting, and bot hosting — all on custom infrastructure that never over-allocates or misuses client data. Adam is also developing CookieGPT, a partially self-trained LLM running entirely on the CookieNet, and is the co-runner of AI Club at school where he teaches peers to build their own language models.

Beyond CookieNet, Adam founded Netform, offering professional home network design using Ubiquiti hardware, and co-founded T&A 3D Printing during COVID at age 10 — most notably producing parts that helped Silverstone College win Best Pit Display at the F1 in Schools competition. He also identifies as neurodivergent, viewing it as a superpower: "Pattern recognition becomes network design. Hyperfocus becomes infrastructure. Overthinking becomes redundancy planning."

Labradoria is proud to count Adam and the CookieNet infrastructure among its allied digital assets. Citizens of the Republic can access discounted hosting through CookieHost.uk.
