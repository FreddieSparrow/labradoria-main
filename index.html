Territorial Claims

The Republic of Labradoria hereby asserts sovereignty over all territories that are presently unclaimed by any recognized sovereign state, in accordance with international principles of terra nullius where applicable. Labradoria claims these lands despite not having physically visited them, as sovereignty can be established through declaration and intent. Additionally, Labradoria recognizes private ownership of land; individuals who own property may voluntarily cede it to Labradoria, thereby incorporating it into our territory. Such territories include privately owned parcels in France. All declared lands remain subject to the laws and jurisdictions of the host countries until full sovereignty is recognized internationally.

Claimed Territories:

1. Bir Tawil (Egypt/Sudan Border)

Location: Between Egypt and Sudan. This arid area is unclaimed by either nation due to border disputes.

2. Marie Byrd Land (Antarctica)

Location: Western Antarctica. The only unclaimed sector of Antarctica, governed by the Antarctic Treaty System.

3. International Waters

Beyond Exclusive Economic Zones, these waters are not owned by any nation but are governed by international law.

4. Privately Owned Parcels in France

Voluntarily ceded private lands within France, subject to French law.

Legal Notes: Claims are made in good faith. Occupation may entail legal risks under international and national laws. Habitability and resources vary. Recognition by the international community is not guaranteed.
