Ministry of Foreign Affairs & Territorial Affairs

Territorial Claims of the Republic

Official Labradoian Territorial Register — Published 2 April 2026

Official Declaration

Declaration of Territorial Sovereignty

The Republic of Labradoria hereby formally asserts and declares sovereign interest over the territories enumerated herein, pursuant to applicable principles of international customary law, including the doctrine of terra nullius where applicable. This declaration is made with full legal intent and is recorded in the official Labradoian Territorial Register.

The Republic of Labradoria recognises and affirms the inviolable right of private property ownership. Any individual or legal entity holding valid title to land under the domestic laws of any jurisdiction in the world may, through a voluntary Instrument of Cession, cede that property to the Republic.

All territories situated within the boundaries of a recognised sovereign state remain fully subject to the domestic laws of that state. Labradoian sovereignty operates concurrently and does not supersede host-nation law.

Territorial Register

Enumerated Claimed Territories

I
Unclaimed Land — Africa

Bir Tawil (Egypt/Sudan Border)

A tract of approximately 2,060 km² claimed by neither the Arab Republic of Egypt nor the Republic of the Sudan, owing to a legal anomaly arising from conflicting boundary conventions of 1899 and 1902. Neither state asserts a claim, as acceptance would require forfeiting a larger adjacent region. Labradoria asserts sovereign interest pursuant to the doctrine of terra nullius.

II
Unclaimed Land — Antarctica

Marie Byrd Land, Antarctica

The sole unclaimed sector of the Antarctic continent, comprising approximately 1.6 million km² of glaciated terrain in West Antarctica. Labradoria asserts declaratory sovereignty while affirming commitment to the peaceful and scientific purposes of the Antarctic Treaty System, to which Labradoria is not a signatory.

III
Maritime Interest — High Seas

International Waters & High Seas

Labradoria asserts its rights as a sovereign state to flag vessels and operate under its jurisdiction on the high seas in accordance with UNCLOS Articles 86–120. No territorial sovereignty over international waters is claimed; these waters are recognised as common heritage of all nations.

IV
Voluntarily Ceded — Worldwide

Voluntarily Ceded Private Land — Any Jurisdiction

Any individual or legal entity holding valid title to privately owned real property under the domestic law of any country in the world may voluntarily cede that property to Labradoria via a formal Instrument of Cession. Ceded parcels are incorporated into the Labradoian Territorial Register. Presently includes privately held parcels within the French Republic. All ceded lands remain fully subject to host-nation domestic law.

Legal Notes

Legal Disclaimers & Notes

  1. Concurrent jurisdiction: All territories situated within the boundaries of a recognised sovereign state remain fully subject to that state's domestic laws. Labradoian sovereignty is concurrent and ceremonial.
  2. Declaration without physical presence: Claims to terra nullius territories are made by formal declaration and recorded notice, consistent with precedent for establishing sovereign intent.
  3. No international recognition presumed: These claims do not presuppose recognition by the United Nations or any member state. Labradoria pursues recognition through diplomatic channels.
  4. Private cessions: Voluntary cessions do not transfer legal title under host-nation law and do not relieve the titleholder of domestic obligations.
  5. Antarctic Treaty: Labradoian activities in the Antarctic region shall be conducted in a manner consistent with the peaceful principles of the Antarctic Treaty System.
